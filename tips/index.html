Good Practices for the Web Developer

Always opt for logic-presentation-separation structural pattern for your project, even small ones. So you can have more control over your code.

  • A request page
  • A class to process the logic
  • Send them back by dispatching to the pagese
  • You can do most codes without a framework

    Separate into apart classes access to databases and other procedures. Setters/getters will allow a better communication between your taks.

    Opt for Inversion Control (Dependency Injection) for larger projects. You can maintain code more easily.
